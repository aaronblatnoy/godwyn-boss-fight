SOULS-LIKE FRAMEWORK
--------------------
Fable's first task on Day 1:
  Search GitHub for "godot 4 souls like" and "godot 4 dark souls"
  Evaluate top results for:
    - Lock-on implementation quality
    - Roll with genuine iframe support
    - Stamina system
    - Animation-driven combat (not purely physics)
    - Active maintenance / Godot 4 compatibility

  If a framework scores well on all five: use it as base.
  If nothing meets the bar: build from scratch using Godot 4's
  CharacterBody3D, AnimationPlayer, and AnimationTree. Combat feel
  is too important to inherit from a poor starting point.

  Priority: correctness of feel over speed of start.

VISUAL DESIGN — PHASE 1
-----------------------
Inspiration: fan art by Enzo Spag and @DOUJ — synthesized below.

  Hair:         Long golden-blonde, partially braided, rest loose and flowing
                Moves with his attacks — dynamic hair physics
  Face:         Noble, warm, open — the face people turned toward
                Expression throughout fight: neutral-sorrowful, the face of
                someone being used who cannot object but carries no aggression
                Clean — no dark markings, no corruption visible
  Armor:        Partial gold plate over open chest — chest exposed, gold
                filigree on the sternum, arm guards, shoulder pieces
                NOT full plate — the body itself is the statement
  Garment:      Long blue flowing robes/cape as primary — same blue as

  Crown:        NONE — no circlet, no crown. His presence is the crown.
  Feet:         Barefoot

  Scale:        3.2m height, 1.4x human proportions — idealized but

                Gold-hilted, blue-tinged blade (subtle — not glowing heavily)

                to grip hard. Mastery expressed through effortlessness.
                NO shield — never defensive. He was merciful, not cautious.

COMBAT PHILOSOPHY — PHASE 1
----------------------------
Fast, smooth, agile chain combos. NOT heavy like Radahn/Godfrey.
NOT erratic like Malenia. Elegant and sustained — a master swordsman.
Every move flows into the next. Recovery windows are short.
The same starting animation can produce 2, 4, or 6 hits depending on
chain extension. Players cannot memorize "X hits then done."
Difficulty target: Promised Consort level or slightly above.

Material:
  Type:               Emissive self-illuminating
  Base color:         Color(0.95, 0.90, 0.82) — pale skin tone, luminous
  Emission:           Color(1.0, 0.88, 0.45) intensity 2.5 (subtler than
                      pure gold — he glows but doesn't overwhelm)
  Emission casts light: YES — Godwyn is the room's light source
  Light radius:       12m
  Light color:        Color(1.0, 0.92, 0.6)
  Robes:              Deep blue, Color(0.08, 0.12, 0.35), no emission
  Armor:              Gold, Color(0.82, 0.65, 0.15), slight metallic sheen

STATS
-----

================================================================================
7. PHASE 1 ATTACKS
================================================================================

COMBAT FEEL
-----------
An incredible swordsman. Not a dancer.

He is relentlessly mobile, continuously pursuing, the sword always in motion.
The fight is one long flowing assault — not a sequence of discrete attacks.
Every move has somewhere to go. Every transition is itself a movement.
He doesn't stop and reset. He redirects.

Players experience him as one organism. The named patterns below are
recognizable shapes within a continuous flow, not separate events with
start and end states.

Design principle: reward aggression, punish hesitation.
"Hesitation is defeat."

Guard: sword hanging LOW at his side, point toward the ground.
This is his only neutral. Everything flows out of it and briefly
returns to it between phrases. It never reads as idle — it reads
as loaded.

Scale: 3.2m. His cuts cover enormous arcs. He doesn't need to chase
because his reach already covers the engagement zone.


STATES
------
His combat system is state-based. He moves between states and each
state has available exits. The fluidity comes from many paths leading
to the same state.

  FACING / LOW HANG — base state. Where initiators begin.

  BACK TO PLAYER — reached via multiple routes (see below).
                   A false window. Every exit from this state is
                   an attack, not a vulnerability.

  AIRBORNE — during jump lunge. Lands into thrust or overshoots.

  EXTENDED / COMMITTED — after overhead slam or straight stab.
                          Brief return to low hang, then next initiator.


CUT VOCABULARY
--------------
His individual cuts. The building blocks everything is made from.

  DIAGONAL \ — top-right sweeping down to bottom-left
  DIAGONAL / — top-left sweeping down to bottom-right
  HORIZONTAL  — wide sweep at chest/head height, either direction
  HORIZONTAL LOW — same sweep but at knee height, different plane
  OVERHEAD SLAM — sword raised, driven straight down, committed
  RISING CUT — sword sweeps low to high, catches forward-rolling players
  STRAIGHT STAB — linear, minimal movement, committed forward
  CIRCULAR — body rotates into the cut, wide coverage, heavier


OVERSHOOT RULE
--------------
Any lunge, thrust, or jump that carries him past the player
immediately transitions to BACK TO PLAYER state.
No reset. The overshoot is never neutral — it becomes the next attack.


THE COMBO TREE
--------------

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
INITIATOR: X COMBO
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
His signature opener. Two crossing diagonals forming a visible X.

  Cut 1 — DIAGONAL \  (top-right to bottom-left)
  Cut 2 — DIAGONAL /  (top-left to bottom-right)

  These cross in the middle of the space in front of him.
  His body is rotating coming out of cut 2. That energy has
  somewhere to go.

  BRANCH A — DOUBLE SPIN
    Two full clockwise rotations, sword extended, wide coverage.
    First spin flows from X momentum. Second spin is the commitment.

  BRANCH B — HALF-SPIN OVERHEAD
    Rotation starts like Branch A — looks like a spin is coming.
    Stops at 180 degrees. Back briefly to player.
    Converts rotational energy into overhead slam straight down.
    The false spin-read is the trap.

  BRANCH C — FULL SPIN → HALF-SPIN OVERHEAD
    Full rotation first (dodge it), then immediately half-spin,
    then overhead. Both. One motion. No reset between them.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
INITIATOR: HORIZONTAL SWEEP R→L
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Wide sweep at chest height, right to left.
Follow-through turns his body. Back faces player.
Enters BACK TO PLAYER state.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
INITIATOR: JUMP LUNGE (gap closer)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Primary gap closer. He reads distance, jumps, covers ground,
comes down into a thrust on landing. No landing animation —
the thrust is the landing.

  Connects: chains immediately into any cut from facing state.
  Overshoots: enters BACK TO PLAYER state.

  Secondary gap closer (to be defined): a fast low-commitment
  dash cut for when he's only one or two steps away.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
FROM BACK TO PLAYER STATE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Reached via: horizontal sweep follow-through, mid-spin,
half-spin stop, any overshoot, or other rotational momentum.

This is not a vulnerability. Every exit is an attack.

  EXIT A — BACKHAND ROTATION
    Counter-clockwise rotation, sword comes around on backhand.
    Catches players who read the turned back as an opening.

    From backhand, branches to:
      → X combo (loops back into X tree)
      → Jump thrust
      → Straight stab (quiet, minimal movement, dangerous)

  EXIT B — OVERHEAD SLAM
    Converts rotation/momentum into committed overhead straight down.

  EXIT C — CONTINUE SPIN
    If he was mid-spin when he entered back-to-player state,
    the spin keeps going. No interruption.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
FROM OVERHEAD SLAM
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Phase 1 (100%–50% HP): one exit only.

  EXIT — FLOW INTO X
    Sword ends at lower-left after impact.
    Rises from lower-left up to upper-left.
    Drives down to lower-right — second diagonal of the X.
    The slam's follow-through became the X entry.
    Players who back off waiting for safety get the X flowing
    out of the move they thought had ended.

Phase 1.5 (below 50% HP): second exit unlocks. See Section 7B.
    TIP INTO GROUND → LIGHTNING AOE added as an additional exit.
    Players who immediately punish the slam get caught mid-punish.
    Both exits now available — ambiguity increases at the threshold.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
DRAGON'S MEMORY — ranged, space control
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Breaks from the melee flow. Forces a spacing conversation.
Players who stay at range get punished for it.

Tell: Godwyn turns 45 degrees away, looks upward (0.55s).
      Spectral golden dragon descends from the void above (0.70s).

Active: dragon exhales sacred cone breath.
  Cone: 110 degrees, 13m length
  Continuous for ~1.1s
  Destroyable: dragon has a small secondary HP bar.
               Attacking and killing the dragon cancels the breath early.
               Rewards aggression toward the dragon.

After breath: Godwyn turns back to face player, continues from
              low hang. Players who ran away are now at range.
              That's where the next initiator begins.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PAUSE — psychological, counter mechanic
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Godwyn stops entirely. Sword low. Looking at the player.
Everything else moves constantly. This doesn't.
That stillness registers as wrong. The arena feels different.

Duration: 3.0–5.0s randomized.

If player attacks:
  He steps offline at 0.12s after input. Already ready.
  Chain begins from the new position immediately.
  No telegraph. He was deciding, not resting.

If player waits:
  He initiates from low hang. Jump lunge weighted +30%.
  The pause was distance assessment.

The Pause cooldown: 15.0s. Rare enough to feel significant.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
MEMORY FRAGMENT — buff trigger (not an attack)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Trigger: 75%, 50%, 25% HP thresholds.

Visual: light flares off him, particles surge, 0.5s burst.
        Miquella's memory intensifying — the template fighting back.

Duration: 10.0s

While active:
  All damage +25%
  Move speed +12%
  Transition time between cycles -15%
  Emission intensity 2.5 → 4.0 — visibly, noticeably brighter

Triggers mid-cycle if HP threshold crossed during active motion.
The hit after the threshold is 25% heavier than the one before it.
Players near thresholds should expect a sudden spike.


================================================================================
7B. PHASE 1 — SOFT PHASE 2 (50% HP THRESHOLD)
================================================================================

At 50% HP, lightning attacks unlock. No cutscene. No pause. No HP reset.
The melee flow continues uninterrupted — the lightning is a new layer
added on top of everything that already exists.

Lore basis: Godwyn the Golden was beloved by the ancient dragons.
They recognized him as kin. The lightning is that inheritance surfacing
as Miquella's memory intensifies — the dragon blood remembering itself.

Visual tell for threshold cross: Memory Fragment fires as normal (light
flares, emission spikes), but this one also produces golden lightning
arcing off his body for 1.5s. Players see the lightning and understand
the fight has changed.

Lightning attacks appear between melee cycles and alongside them.
They are not a separate mode — they integrate into the existing flow.
He can finish a melee cycle and immediately call down strikes.
He can use the shrinking circle to reset engagement range before a
jump lunge. The two systems feed each other.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
PLACIDUSAX CALL — patterned strikes
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Godwyn raises his sword overhead. Golden lightning answers it,
striking down in a deliberate pattern across the arena floor.

Tell: sword arm raises slowly, golden light accumulates at the tip.
      Pattern markers appear on the ground 0.6s before strikes land
      (same visual language as Elden Ring AoE warnings — faint gold rings).

Pattern: not random. A readable arrangement with clear safe zones.
         Multiple strikes, staggered 0.2s apart across the pattern.
         Players who read the safe zone survive. Players who panic-dodge
         into a strike zone don't.

After the pattern resolves: Godwyn brings sword down and immediately
re-enters melee flow. The call is a spacing tool, not a break.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
TARGETED STRIKES — forced movement
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Two versions: standalone and mid-melee.

STANDALONE VERSION
Lightning specifically tracks the player's current position.
Series of strikes that arrive where you ARE, not where you're going.
Standing still is death. Constant movement is survival.

Tell: Godwyn looks directly at the player, sword held out to the side.
      Gold light pulses from the blade tip before each strike.

Behavior: 3–5 sequential strikes, each targeting player's position
          at moment of the pulse. 0.4s between pulses.
          Each strike has a small AoE radius — staying just outside
          isn't enough. Real distance is required.

After final strike: he closes distance. Jump lunge weighted heavily.
Players who've been running from the lightning are now at range.

MID-MELEE VERSION (Phase 1.5 integration)
A single targeted bolt woven into the melee flow. No standalone
sequence — just one strike called down at the player's position
during or between cuts. His melee rhythm doesn't stop for it.

Tell: brief gold pulse at sword tip mid-combo. 0.25s warning.
      Shorter than standalone tell — less time to react.

The player is already dodging melee and suddenly has to also move
from their current position. The two pressures overlap.
This is the "lighter" lightning that bleeds into the melee.
Frequency: can appear once per melee cycle, not every cycle.
Weighted toward appearing when the player has been stationary
for more than 0.5s — punishing players who found a safe spot
and stopped moving.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
HORIZONTAL LIGHTNING SWEEP — full arena coverage
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Used when the player is at distance. Punishes passive spacing.

Tell: Godwyn extends his sword arm to his right, fully extended,
      at chest height. Sword horizontal. He is facing the player
      but the sword is pointed away. 0.5s hold.

Active: he drags the arm slowly right to left.
        Parallel lines of golden lightning extend from the blade
        perpendicular to the sweep direction, traveling outward
        as the arm moves. The lines follow the sword's path,
        sweeping the full arena.

Coverage: entire arena width. No safe side — the sweep goes all the way.
          Safe option: dodge THROUGH a gap between lightning lines.
          Timing a roll through the sweep is the intended counter-play.
          The slowness is deliberate — panic-dodging early puts you in
          the next line.

After sweep: arm drops. He advances. The sweep created distance;
             he immediately begins closing it.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
SHRINKING CIRCLE — arena compression, passive player punishment
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
A ring of golden lightning appears simultaneously around both
Godwyn and the player. It contracts inward at a steady rate.

The only safe space is close to him.
Distance kills you. The arena enforces it.

Tell: lightning crackles along the ground in a ring before it
      solidifies. 0.8s warning before the circle becomes lethal.

Behavior:
  Circle starts at ~15m radius from center point between them.
  Contracts at ~1.5m/s.
  Lethal on contact — not a damage-over-time, a hard hit.
  Full contraction to ~4m radius takes ~7s.
  At 4m radius, circle holds for 2s then disperses.

During the circle: Godwyn does not wait. He continues his melee
flow while the circle shrinks. Players are being compressed toward
him while he's already swinging.

After dispersal: he re-enters normal flow. The circle was geometry,
                 not a break in his behavior.

Design intention: punishes passive players mechanically and literally.
                  The shrinking circle and the melee flow combine —
                  you're being pushed toward a man who isn't stopping.
                  "Hesitation is defeat" made environmental.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
DRAGON'S CHARGE — one-time cinematic attack
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Happens once per fight. Not repeatable. Weighted to trigger sometime
in Phase 1.5 when the player has been alive long enough to have
settled into a rhythm. The disruption is the point.

SETUP — he creates distance.
  He shoves or sweeps the player back — a deliberate distancing move,
  not part of a melee chain. Player is knocked back several meters.
  He steps back simultaneously, widening the gap between them.
  Arena is now open. He is at one end. Player at the other.

EMERGENCE
  The ground behind him cracks. A spectral golden dragon erupts
  upward from beneath the arena floor — rising fast, wings spread.
  It ascends directly behind him, framed against the void sky.
  He doesn't look at it. He's looking at the player.

THE CHARGE
  The dragon levels off above him and surges forward —
  straight toward the player, covering the full arena length.
  As it flies, it breathes fire downward along the exact line
  of its flight path. The fire traces the ground in a continuous
  beam from Godwyn's position all the way to the far end of the arena.

  If the player is in front of him: sprint hard left or right.
  The fire line covers the entire forward corridor.
  No dodge roll survives it — roll iframes aren't enough lateral
  distance. The counter is to RUN, not dodge.

  Width of fire line: ~4m — wide enough that cautious movement
  doesn't clear it. Real commitment to a direction is required.

THE LANDING
  Dragon passes through and disperses at the far end of the arena —
  dissolves back into golden light.
  Godwyn closes distance immediately after.
  No recovery window after the charge. He's already coming.

ONE-TIME FLAG
  Remove from pool after use. Does not repeat.
  This is a moment, not a mechanic.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERHEAD SLAM EXIT — TIP INTO GROUND → LIGHTNING AOE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Unlocks at 50% HP as a second exit from the overhead slam.
See Section 7 (FROM OVERHEAD SLAM) for context on the ambiguity.

After the overhead slam lands, instead of flowing into X:
he drives the sword tip into the ground. Holds it. Beat of silence.
Looks like the move is over. Players who immediately punish eat this.

Golden lightning erupts from the impact point.
  Radius: 8m from impact
  Damage: significant — this is a punish for punishing
  Visual: lightning travels outward along ground cracks from tip

After eruption: sword pulls free, returns to low hang, melee continues.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
LIGHTNING FOLLOW-UPS — melee integration
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Lightning moves feed back into the melee tree, not into idle.

After PLACIDUSAX CALL: re-enters from low hang, any initiator.
After TARGETED STRIKES (standalone): jump lunge weighted heavily.
After TARGETED STRIKES (mid-melee): melee cycle continues unbroken.
After HORIZONTAL SWEEP: advances immediately, X combo or jump lunge.
After SHRINKING CIRCLE disperses: player is now at close range.
                                   He's already there. Melee continues.
After LIGHTNING AOE: sword pulls free, melee resumes from low hang.

The lightning is a spacing system. The melee is the pressure system.
The two work together — lightning controls where the player is,
melee punishes them for being there.
